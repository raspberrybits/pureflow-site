Filtration

Step 1

The first step in our filtration process involves a microfiltration membrane that removes larger particles, such as sand, sediment, and other debris. This membrane has a pore size of 0.1 microns, which means that it can remove particles that are 100 times smaller than a human hair. As the water passes through the membrane, these larger particles are trapped and removed, ensuring that the water is free of any visible impurities.

Purification

Step 2

After the initial filtration stage, the water enters the purification stage, where it passes through an activated carbon filter. This filter removes any chemicals, such as chlorine or pesticides, that may be present in the water. The activated carbon works by adsorbing these chemicals onto its surface, effectively removing them from the water. This stage also removes any unpleasant odors or tastes that may be present in the water.

Sterilization

Step 3

The final stage in our filtration process involves the use of a sterilization filter that eliminates any harmful bacteria or viruses that may be present in the water. This filter utilizes an advanced technology that uses ultraviolet light to kill any remaining microorganisms in the water. This process ensures that the water is safe to drink and free from any harmful pathogens.
